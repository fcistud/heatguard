Data & research

What it runs on — and what is missing

The physics runs on free, open code plus free reanalysis weather. The standards that define the science are mostly paywalled (but reimplemented in open libraries). And granular Gulf worker exposure and health data is essentially a public void — the single biggest gap, which HeatGuard names rather than hides.

ℹ️ How sources are tagged

Access is tagged free free+reg (free with registration/licence) paid restricted (no usable open access). Findings were gathered by web research and then adversarially verified; corrections that survived are folded in and genuine uncertainties are flagged.

⚠️ Honest deviation

The experienced-worker ramp ([0.50, 1.00] over 2 days) is more permissive than NIOSH on days 2–3 (NIOSH: ≤20%/day, reaching 100% by day 5). It is a deliberate simplification, flagged here. To own the full ISO set (7243 + 7933 + 8996 + 9920) costs ≈ CHF 645 — none of it needed to run the engine, but conformance auditing would need the authoritative texts.

The Nicaragua intervention evidence #

HeatGuard's impact model rests on the La Isla Network Adelante Initiative at Ingenio San Antonio (ISA), Nicaragua. The effect sizes are real and peer-reviewed — but more specific than a flat multiplier implies.

SourceUseNotes
Hansson et al. 2025, OEM (kidney)uses-now~1,044 cane workers. The "94%" is the decline in incident kidney injury among the highest-risk subgroup (burned cane cutters), harvest 1 → 3/4 — not a whole-workforce effect. The narrower AKI rate fell ~60% (20→8 per 1,000 worker-months).
Hansson et al. 2024, Ann. Work Exp. Health (productivity)uses-nowThe 10–20% band rounds subgroup gains (~9% burned cane cutters, ~19% seed cutters), measured harvest-over-harvest.
Schlader et al. 2025, Ann. Global Health (economics)uses-now⚠️ Documented mill-hospital AKI treatment cost = $253.48 (our economics.json uses $1,200). ROI framings: $1.02/$1 5-yr average, negative in years 1–2.
ILO — Heat at work (2024)uses-nowSecondary repackaging of the LIN studies (the 94% / 10–20% are not independent of the papers above).

The honest gaps — what is not available #

🚫 Real voids, not oversights
  • No public hourly Gulf WBGT dataset. The only open global WBGT grids (Brimicombe/Zenodo) are daily and end 2021. WBGT is modelled from reanalysis, never measured on-site.
  • No public Gulf WBGT-meter ground truth. Qatar mandates WBGT-based stoppage but doesn't publish the underlying site measurements; no GCC state releases instrument time-series.
  • ERA5-HEAT has no WBGT (UTCI + MRT only) — no official reanalysis WBGT variable exists.
  • Near-total Gulf worker-exposure evidence void. A 2025 systematic review found only 1 of 19 migrant-worker heat studies (2,293 workers) came from the entire Gulf region.
  • No open individual-level Gulf exposure dataset; no public Gulf worker sweat-rate or fluid-balance dataset.
  • Adult-male anthropometry is largely missing from DHS (recent rounds focus on women 15–49 and under-5s) — so body-mass priors for the overwhelmingly male workforce are thin.
  • No open AKI/CKDu incidence registry for Gulf migrant workers; evidence is fragmentary.
  • Migrant heat-death data is structurally unavailable. The oft-cited ~10,000 deaths/year is all-cause, many certified as "cardiac arrest" without autopsy.
  • Primary legal texts are hard to ingest. Government portals block automated fetch; fine amounts aren't always officially published; windows/seasons drift year to year.
  • The standards are paywalled (ISO ≈ CHF 645; ACGIH tables copyrighted) — fine for running the engine, a constraint for formal conformance auditing.

Bottom line

The science and the weather are free and open; the worker-side ground truth for the Gulf is the gap — which is exactly why HeatGuard pairs an estimate with a measured WBGT path and treats its impact assumptions as tunable, sensitivity-tested inputs rather than settled facts.
